Influenza Background

Influenza (more commonly known as the flu) is a common infectious disease caused by a virus. This is a very bad disease because it infects a lot of people and it is very easily transferred. It also changes every years which is why you need a new vaccine every year. The flu can be serious or even deadly for elderly people, newborn babies, and people with certain chronic illnesses.



Symptoms of the flu appear out of no where and are worse than the common cold. Symptoms may include;

  • Fatigue
  • Chills
  • Runny nose
  • Sore throat
  • Cough
  • Headache
  • Body or muscle aches
  • Fever

Fun Facts:

  • Between 5% and 20% of people in the U.S. get the flu each year.

  • To figure out if you have the flu or a cold, just know that the flu causes headaches and fevers and colds don't.
